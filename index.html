After a brief career of working in digital marketing and my bachelor studies in communication sciences I quickly found my passion for building tech with great people.
Had a great 4 year ride leading the @connectsocial.si dev team and was fortunate enough to work with startups from all over the globe.
I'm used to working in agile environments with version control, automated testing, continuous integration, mostly navigating in the fullstack world of Javascript.

As my career evolves I find myself drawn to research about psychology of engineering teams, namely what makes quality engineers motivated to work together with a common goal over a longer period of time.

In the world where number of active engineers doesn't come close to filling the needs of the engineering workload and quality engineers are increasingly hard to find and even harder to keep, I sadly still see organizations missing the key points in team management:

Even though we are engineers we are still only human beings with emotions.

Many leaders understand their role as handing out tasks and making sure they get done on time.

My approach has always been different. The hard part of leadership is to create a bond with each employee, figure out what makes him or her tick, what drives them to get up every day, come to work and fix bugs and implement features.

After you figure that out, the real work begins. You get to continuously work on fitting those needs to your organization's structure.
For me that motivation has always been teamwork, collaboration and just straight up enjoying building products that help people achieve a goal.

If you think that this kind of approach is not worth the hustle, consider waking up one day and figuring out that your team decided to pursue their goals with an employer that actually understands this.

I'm generally interested in long term positions such as team lead, engineering manager or CTO.
